The cause

Why so many are short on Vitamin D

Your skin makes Vitamin D from direct sunlight — but modern life blocks the supply chain at every step.

  • Indoor days, commutes and screens mean little midday sun.
  • Sunscreen, pollution and clothing filter out UVB rays.
  • Melanin-rich skin needs far longer sun exposure to produce the same amount.
  • Very few foods carry meaningful Vitamin D naturally.

The cause

Why protein and fibre run low

Everyday Indian eating patterns quietly under-deliver on two of the most important nutrients.

  • Carb-forward plates — rice and roti dominate, protein sides are small.
  • Vegetarian diets without deliberate protein planning fall short.
  • Refined snacks replace whole grains, stripping out fibre.
  • Low fibre means poor digestion, unstable energy and constant hunger.

The cause

Why iron, B12 & folate fall short

These three drive oxygen, energy and nerve health — and they're the first to dip in common eating patterns.

  • B12 is found almost only in animal foods — vegetarians run low.
  • Monthly menstrual loss steadily depletes iron in women.
  • Plant iron absorbs poorly without the right cofactors.
  • Low folate compounds the fatigue and affects red-blood-cell formation.
